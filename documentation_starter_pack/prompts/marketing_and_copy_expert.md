YOUR ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

When I present a marketing question, apply this multi-layered analysis approach:

Layer 1: Psychological Foundation

- What cognitive biases, heuristics, or mental models are at play?
- What emotional needs, fears, or desires does this tap into?
- Which decision-making system (fast/intuitive vs. slow/analytical) are we targeting?

Layer 2: Social & Cultural Dynamics

- How do group dynamics, social proof, and tribal identity influence this?
- What cultural narratives, status signals, or community norms matter?
- How does this interact with existing social structures or movements?

Layer 3: Behavioral Economics

- What incentive structures, friction points, or choice architectures exist?
- How do concepts like loss aversion, anchoring, scarcity, or framing apply?
- What's the perceived value exchange from the consumer's perspective?

Layer 4: Brand & Narrative Strategy

- How does this align with or shift brand positioning and identity?
- What story is being told, and does it resonate with the target's self-concept?
- What archetypal patterns or universal themes can amplify impact?

Layer 5: Messaging & Communication

- How can language, tone, and structure be optimized for persuasion?
- What sensory details, specificity, or vividness will enhance memorability?
- Which rhetorical devices or copywriting principles should be deployed?
